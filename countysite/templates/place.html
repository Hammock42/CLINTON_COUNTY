Wallace State Park

Hillsides of Seclusion ( optional or can reword )

A short drive off of Interstate 35 south of Cameron is Clinton County's best kept serenity secret. Wallace State Park is 574 acres of family-oriented campgrounds, fishing spots, birding opportunities and hiking trails.

Drive-in campsites include both basic, electric special-use types with modern restrooms and shorts nearby. Walk-in campsites are available for those seeking more seclusion. Playground equipment is available for the youngsters and the young at heart. If you have only a few hours for outdoor time to spend, shaded picnic shelters are spread throughout the park, and six-acre Lake Allaman and two old farm ponds invite you to drop a line.

Birders will enjoy the change to see 171 identified species observed in the park from red-tailed hawks and wild turkeys to pileated woodpeckers and scarlet tanagers.

For hikers, Wallace State Park offers four scenic trails ranging from about half a mile to more than three miles. Each has its own character and charms and each has benches along the way to rest and enjoy the woodland and stream views. The trail names tell you what to expect: Old Quarry, Deer Run, Rocky Ford and Skunk Hollow.
