Lathrop Living History Festival
Step back into the 1800s at the Lathrop Antique Show Ground's Annual Living History Festival! There's nothing like it in northwest Missouri.

For THREE full days, (second weekend in October each year) enjoy immersive experiences of the past —stir apple butter, dip candles, churn butter, shuck corn, crush rocks, watch the process of making sorghum, take home a printed card from an antique printing press, indulge in delicious apple fritters and apple cider.

Take a class in the Walnut Grove One Room School and play old fashioned games in the schoolyard. Enjoy teepees and other demonstrations from the Indian Council of Many Nations. You can even get a haircut in the old Barber Shop and learn to spit sunflower seeds in the nearby spittoon. And don't forget to stop by the General Store for old-fashioned goodies and much, much more!
