Trice-Dedman Memorial Woods
Explore the Timeless Beauty of Clinton County

Tucked between Lathrop and Plattsburg along Highway 116, the Trice-Dedman Memorial Woods offers a rare glimpse into Nature's past. This 60-acre sanctuary, maintained by the Nature Conservancy, is one of Clinton County's treasurers. It preserves one of the few remaining examples of upland oak woodlands that once covered much of northwest Missouri.

A Walk Through History

Wander through old-growth white oak trees, some more than 180 years old, and marvel at the boulder-strewn hills and exposed limestone bedrock along the streams. Two hiking trails of low-to-moderate difficulty let you explore the quiet, untouched beauty of these woodlands. Admission is free, making it a perfect spot for a peaceful retreat.

A Naturalist's Paradise

With nearly 200 native plant species, the woods come alive with vibrant spring flowers like violets, trout lilies, and Dutchman's breeches. Lucky visitors might even spot rare species like orchids or dwarf larkspur. The woodland's timeless charm and diversity offer a haven for nature lovers, hikers, and photographers alike.

Know Before You Go

For a safe and enjoyable experience, the Nature Conservancy recommends wearing long pants, hiking boots, and bringing essentials like water, insect repellent, and binoculars. In warmer months, light-colored, breathable clothing is advised. Please respect the natural environment by staying on marked trails and leaving no trace of your visit.
