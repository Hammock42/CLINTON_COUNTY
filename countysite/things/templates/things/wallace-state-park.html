Wallace State Park
"Nature's Hidden Gem in Clinton County"
Discover Wallace State Park

Just a quick drive off Interstate 35, south of Cameron, lies one of Missouri's best-kept secrets: Wallace State Park, a tranquil 574-acre haven of wooded trails, serene meadows, and family-friendly campsites. Whether you're seeking a peaceful weekend getaway or just a few hours to reconnect with Nature, this park has something for everyone.

Outdoor Adventure for All

Pitch your tent at one of the drive-in campsites with options for basic or electric hookups and nearby modern restrooms and showers. For those craving solitude, walk-in sites provide a more secluded experience. Kids will love the playground, and families can gather in the shady picnic shelters, making Wallace an ideal spot for day trips or extended stays.

Fishing, Birding, and Hiking

Lake Allaman and two charming farm ponds offer perfect spots to cast a line, and the park's 171 bird species—from red-tailed hawks to vibrant scarlet tanagers—make it a birder's paradise. Hikers can enjoy four scenic trails, each with its own unique character. Old Quarry, Rocky Ford or Skunk Hollow trails are about 1 mile each and vary in difficulty. The longer Deer Run trail stretches for 3 miles. Whichever you choose, benches along the way await to let you pause and savor the beauty of the woodland streams.
