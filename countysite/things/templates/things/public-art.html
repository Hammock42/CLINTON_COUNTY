Explore Clinton County's Vibrant Public Art Scene

Public Art in Clinton County

Public art comes alive in Clinton County, where murals, sculptures, and restored ghost signs tell the stories of our past and celebrate our present. Stroll through Plattsburg and discover its artistic treasures, starting with the large mural at Maple and Main, which depicts the historic Jefferson Highway. Across the street, a beautiful mural honors the county's connection to nature, focusing on the importance of birds. Just up the road at Main and Broadway, another mural captures the charm of rural life.

Cameron's Railroad Legacy

In Cameron, history is brought to life with a stunning mural at Walnut and E. Third, featuring a steam locomotive chugging through the landscape, paying homage to the town's railroad legacy.

Plattsburg's Artistic Highlights

Public art also soars at the DeFreece Real Estate building in Plattsburg, where metal birds take flight above the streets. In front of the county courthouse, you'll find the iconic statue of David Rice Atchison, famously claimed to have been U.S. president for a day. The exterior of the courthouse itself is home to whimsical art, including a painting of the original courthouse for Missouri's bicentennial and vintage black-and-white photos of Main Street from a century ago.

Ghost Signs Revived

Ghost signs—vintage advertisements painted on buildings—have been brought back to life across the county. In Plattsburg, check out the restored signs for General Arthur cigars and Streiff, Peterman & Co. Studebaker buggies. In Lathrop, a long-hidden sign for Mail Pouch Tobacco was recently rediscovered on Oak Street.
